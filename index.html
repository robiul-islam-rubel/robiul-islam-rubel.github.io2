About me

I am a second year Ph.D. student in the Department of Computer Science at William & Mary. Currently, I am working under the guidence of Prof. Trey Woodlief. My research interests lie at the intersection of Software Engineering, Autonomous Driving Safety and Application of Large Language Models(LLMs).

Before beginning of my Ph.D. journey, I was actively engaged in competitive programming and solved more than 1500 algorithmic problems and participated in various programming contests including the ICPC Dhaka Regional. Alogside my academic pursuits, I also served as a part-time lecturer at Uttara University and held the role of President of Uttara University Programming Club (UUPC), where I worked to foster a culture of problem-solving and programming excellence among students.
